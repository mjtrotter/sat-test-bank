# Skill Taxonomy — Vanguard Adaptive Trainer
> Synthesized: 2026-03-28 from 11 source textbook TOCs + competition structure

## Overview

Six primary domains, each with 3+ ELO-gated levels. Level 1 in every domain corresponds to strong 6th-grade / early competition skills. Level 3 represents hard MATHCOUNTS National / AMC 10 territory. Levels 4+ extend to AMC 12 / AIME for students who blow through the core progression.

The Batterson "Competition Math for Middle School" provides the skeleton for the MVP (middle school competition math). AoPS and Gelfand texts provide depth for higher levels and lesson scaffolding material.

## Cross-Domain Skill Tags

Problems often span multiple domains. The `secondary_skills` array handles this:
- A geometry problem requiring algebraic manipulation → primary: Geometry, secondary: [quadratic_equations]
- A counting problem using modular arithmetic → primary: Counting, secondary: [modular_arithmetic]
- A number theory problem requiring casework → primary: Number Theory, secondary: [casework]

## Diagnostic Placement

The baseline diagnostic should sample across all 6 domains at Levels 1-3. Suggested composition:
- 8 questions per domain × 6 domains = 48 questions (close to the 50-question fallback in GAPS.md)
- 3 questions at Level 1, 3 at Level 2, 2 at Level 3 per domain
- Adaptive shortcutting: if a student gets all Level 1 questions right in a domain, skip to Level 2-3 items
